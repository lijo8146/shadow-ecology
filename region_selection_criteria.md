# Shadow Ecology Study Region Selection Criteria

**Status:** LOCKED Month 1, Week 2
**Purpose:** Define, in writing and before any data pull, the criteria used to select the Shadow Ecology study region. No candidate region may be added or removed after this document is locked without a dated amendment.

## Criteria

### (a) Subsurface hydrology drives vegetation
The candidate region must exhibit a plausible, mechanistic link between subsurface hydrologic conditions (depth-to-water, groundwater dynamics) and vegetation pattern not primarily geology, soils, or irrigation-driven signal. This is required for residual structure to be interpretable as a hydrologic signal rather than a confound.

### (b) Heterogeneous monitoring density, including Tribal lands
The candidate region must contain a genuine contrast between well-instrumented areas and areas of chronic monitoring sparsity, with Tribal lands represented among the sparsely monitored areas of interest. This heterogeneity is what makes residual patterns diagnostically useful for identifying monitoring gaps.

### (c) Freely accessible imagery
The candidate region must have full, reliable coverage from freely available imagery programs (Sentinel-2, Landsat, and ideally PlanetScope via NASA CSDA), with manageable cloud cover and no access barriers.

## Candidate Regions

- Rio Grande Basin (primary candidate)
- Colorado Plateau (pre-validated alternative)
- Great Basin (pre-validated alternative)

## Process

1. Month 1: comparative evidence gathered for all three candidates against criteria (a)–(c) via imagery inventory, groundwater/monitoring network inventories, and literature review.
2. Month 1, Week 4: Region Selection Report drafted, comparing candidates against criteria.
3. Month 2, Week 5: final region selection locked based on Month 1 evidence. **No revisiting after this point.**
